## Bio:

Amir Yazdavar is a researcher at the Data Semantics Lab Research Center at Kansas State University in Manhattan, KS. He is broadly interested in Data Science, Deep Learning, Machine Learning, Statistical Analysis, Inference, Data Analytics, Hypothesis Testing, Knowledge Graphs, Knowledge Engineering, Natural Language Processing, Information Extraction, Medical Informatics, and Big Data.

His research applies multidisciplinary methods to glean insights from data.

Previously, he worked as a data/research scientist at Weill Cornell Medicine (Cornell University), the National Institute of Health (NIH), and the Information Science Institute (ISI).
